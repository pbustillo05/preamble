For $m \geq 0$, we let $[m]$ be the stratified line with $m$ points.

\begin{definition} We define $\Bord^{fr}(X, m_1, \ldots, m_n)$ to be the space whose points are the data of:
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item a framed open manifolds $(M^n, \eta)$ where $\eta\in \{\bullet\} \simeq \tau_M \in M \to BO(n)$,
        \item a continuous map $\pi \in M \to X \times \prod_{i=1}^n [m_i]$,
        \item a trivialization of $M$ over each strata.
    \end{enumerate}

    A path in the space should be a map of manifolds over $X$ (\textbf{not} over $\prod_{i=1}^n [m_i]$) which "preserves infinite directions" in a way to be precisely defined.
\end{definition}
